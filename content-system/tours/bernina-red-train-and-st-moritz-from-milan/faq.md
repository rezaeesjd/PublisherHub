# FAQ

## How long is this tour?
Approximately 12 to 13 hours, departing Milan at 07:15.

## Where is the meeting point in Milan?
Milan Visitor Centre at Largo Cairoli 18, on the corner of Via Cusani and Largo Cairoli (metro MM1 Cairoli / MM2 Lanza). Look for staff in fuchsia shirts.

## Do I need a passport?
Yes. The source explicitly states "A VALID PASSPORT IS REQUIRED to join this tour" because the route crosses into Switzerland.

## What's the cancellation policy?
Free cancellation is available up to 24 hours before departure. Cancellations within 24 hours are non-refundable.

## Is this tour wheelchair accessible?
No. The source states the tour is not suitable for guests with wheelchairs or impaired mobility.

## What's included?
A 2nd-class Bernina Express ticket (with opening windows), roundtrip coach transportation, expert tour leader, and free time in St Moritz.

## What's the route?
Milan → Tirano (short stop) → Bernina Express scenic crossing → Alp Grüm panoramic stop → Bernina Diavolezza glacier viewpoint → Pontresina village → St Moritz free time → Lake St Moritz → Bernina Express return to Milan.

## What happens with last-minute bookings?
Bookings made less than 12 hours before departure cannot be guaranteed. A valid email and active phone number are required at booking so the operator can reach you with confirmation and updates.

## What languages are offered?
English is listed as the guide and service language.

## Are kids welcome?
Yes — pricing bands are Adult 12–50, Child 4–11, Infant 0–3. Infants are not required to sit on laps and infant seats are available.
