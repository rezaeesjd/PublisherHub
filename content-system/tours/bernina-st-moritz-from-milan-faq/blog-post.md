# Bernina Express + St Moritz from Milan: Your Questions Answered

Planning a full-day Swiss Alps trip from Milan raises a lot of practical questions before you commit. This page answers the ones that come up most often — passport rules, what happens if you miss the deadline, cancellation terms, and what the route actually covers — so you can book with confidence.

---

## Do I need a passport for this tour?

Yes. A valid passport is required. The Bernina route crosses the Italian–Swiss border between Tirano and the Swiss canton of Graubünden, so every traveller must carry a full passport. EU identity cards are not a substitute for this tour. Check your passport expiry before booking.

## What is the cancellation policy?

All bookings are non-refundable — the operator's policy is "all sales final." There is no free cancellation window. Confirm your travel dates before completing the booking, and factor this into any travel insurance decision you make.

## How long is the tour and when does it leave?

The tour runs approximately 12 to 13 hours. Departure from Milan is at 07:15. You should be at the meeting point a few minutes before that.

## Where exactly do we meet in Milan?

The meeting point is the Milan Visitor Centre at Largo Cairoli 18, on the corner of Via Cusani and Largo Cairoli. The nearest metro stops are MM1 Cairoli and MM2 Lanza. Look for staff wearing fuchsia shirts.

## Can I book last minute?

Bookings made less than 12 hours before the 07:15 departure cannot be guaranteed. If you book close to the deadline, provide a valid email address and an active phone number so the operator can reach you with final confirmation before the tour departs.

## Is this tour accessible for guests with mobility needs?

No. The tour is not suitable for guests who use wheelchairs or have impaired mobility. The itinerary includes mountain train platforms and stops at alpine viewpoints that require comfortable walking. If you have any doubts, contact the operator before booking.

## What is the route?

The day follows the UNESCO Bernina Express railway from Tirano into the Swiss Alps and back. Key stops are:

- **Tirano** — short town stop before boarding the train
- **Bernina Express scenic crossing** — 90 minutes through high alpine terrain
- **Alp Grüm** — panoramic viewpoint at 2,091 m
- **Diavolezza glacier station** — 1-hour stop with glacier views
- **Pontresina** — 1-hour stop in an Engadine village
- **St Moritz and Lake St Moritz** — approximately 2 hours and 20 minutes of free time
- **Return journey** — 3-hour Bernina Express ride back to Tirano, then coach to Milan

## What's included?

Your booking covers a 2nd-class Bernina Express ticket with opening windows, roundtrip coach transport between Milan and Tirano, an expert English-speaking tour leader for the full day, and free time in St Moritz.

## How many people are on the tour?

The group is capped at 20 travellers, which keeps the experience manageable at viewpoints and during town stops.

## Does the tour run every day?

Yes — the tour operates daily, Monday through Sunday.

## Are children welcome?

Yes. Pricing is structured across three age bands: adult (12–50), child (4–11), and infant (0–3). Infant seats are available. Infants do not need to travel on laps.
