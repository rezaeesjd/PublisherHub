# Swiss Alps from Milan: Bernina Express Route Guide

If you want a classic Swiss Alps day from Milan, the Bernina route is one of the strongest options: it combines mountain rail scenery, high-altitude viewpoints, and time in St Moritz in a single long day.

## Why this route stands out

The route typically passes through Tirano, climbs the UNESCO Bernina railway corridor, and includes iconic Alpine sections around Alp Grüm, Diavolezza, and Pontresina before St Moritz. For many travelers, it offers the best scenery-to-effort ratio from Milan.

## Typical route flow (guided day-trip format)

1. Early departure from Milan (around 07:15 for many guided departures).
2. Transfer to Tirano and board the Bernina rail section.
3. Scenic crossings and mountain panoramas (including Alp Grüm/Diavolezza area).
4. Time in Pontresina and St Moritz.
5. Evening return to Milan.

Exact timing can vary by season, traffic, and operating conditions.
